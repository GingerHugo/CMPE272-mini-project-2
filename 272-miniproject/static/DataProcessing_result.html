    Part IV:

    Conclusion

    After analysis, we have found that the life expectancy and health expenditure are slightly positively correlated which indicates that keeping healthy does not mean paying a lot. And other factors can be found for the long life expectancy.
     
            
    From the second analysis, we can see that HIV/AIDS has little effects on people's life expectancy. This result infers that some worries and fears about HIV/AIDS are over-exaggerated. If prevented properly and treated in time, HIV/AIDS is not that dangerous.
    
            
    The most interesting thing is that the number of HIV/AIDS sufferings and health expenditure is uncorrelated. Two preliminary conclusions can be made: one is that the share on HIV/AIDS treatment in the total health expenditure is small and the other is the present HIV/AIDS prevention methods are not that effective no matter how much we invest in. So more data and surveys can be made in this issue to figure out more effective ways to fight the HIV/AIDS.
